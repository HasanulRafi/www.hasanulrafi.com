One True Genius.

Let's talk about a genius. Today I want to talk about someone who has over 100 patents and more than 700 innovations. This is a brief introduction to a mesmerizingly curious human mind Nikola Tesla.No one gallery could contain all the tech genius the world has ever seen, but here's a start.What is common in Pythagoras, Galileo, Archimedes, DaVinci, Einstein, Newton, Tesla, Edison, Henry Ford, Louis Pasteur,Steve Jobs and Elon musk?

Many of them were centuries ahead of their time. In my eyes when it comes to geniuses you cannot distinguish or compare between them. Every one of them had a different role in the improvement and modernization throughout the course of history, which created the rapid advancement of our civilization over the centuries. The perfect and imperfect events turned them into the person they are with an incredible mind and it all came to them in a natural way.

We all heard of the fairytale of Edison but very few of us will recognize the father of modern technology Nikola tesla. Edison was less electrified than Nikola Tesla, it is the inventor who made alternating-current electric power possible. The Austrian Empire or modern-day Croatia Tesla's birthday was during a particularly violent lightning storm reading this as a bad omen the midwife said to Tesla's mother that Tesla would be a child of darkness but his mother immediately replied no he will be a child of light.

This statement was significant from an early age, it was clear that Tesla was a remarkable kid from the beginning. He went to an engineering school but was not prepared for the final exams because he was addicted to gambling and that's how he dropped and never graduated but he had the burning desire to create new things in his laboratory and only used to sleep 2 hours a night. He started his career under Edison's company modern-day General Electric. Tesla wasn't only theoretical but In terms of practical discoveries that are the basis for the technologies we use each and every day it was Nikola Tesla who was the most brilliant of all he had a mind of the rarest kind he can memorize entire books and visualize inventions with pinpoint accuracy once he had the concept of an invention fully visualized he then undertook the hard work to bring it to reality in comparison most other scientists and engineers unwittingly narrow-minded in their thinking ideas and innovations. In his long career, he never got married because he thought it's a distraction and devoted himself to research and quoted "I do not think you can name many great inventions that have been made by married men."

He could become one of the wealthiest men at that time and he refused to take money from banks and man of them used him for their own benefits. He never cared about money and he only cares about the improvement of humankind. He was obsessed with 3,6,9 and used to have a problem with OCD disorder.

Tesla's research patents and theoretical work formed the basis of modern alternating current which put him into direct competition with Edisonâ€™s DC current. Edison tried his soul out to prove Tesla wrong that AC current is dangerous for living things and he killed animals to prove his claim. Tesla electrified himself with AC current in a world stage to light a bulb. Which made a celebrity overnight and he gained prestige for his work worldwide. He worked on magnetism and found SI unit magnetic flux density or "magnetic induction B field". Aside from his work on AC current, electromagnetism, electromechanical engineering, Tesla created the basis of modern-day robotics, remote control, radar, ballistic, nuclear physics, theoretical physics, and computer science technology.

Many of his innovations have been stolen and in 1943 Supreme Court of the United States credited him as being the inventor of the radio. He once said, " Most of my happy memories are in my laboratory". His works were so good that we use them every single day.He had a mental break down in his late-life from denial of his innovations and eventually died poor with depression and loneliness at the age of 86.I want to finish this with one of his thoughtful quotes "The scientists of today think deeply instead of clearly. One must be sane to think clearly, but one can think deeply and be quite insane. Our virtues and our failings are inseparable, like force and matter. When they separate, man is no more."For more information please read the book - Nikola Tesla: Imagination and the man invented the 20th century by Sean Patrick.
